# Risk Likelihood and Impact Matrix

Technology risk assessments commonly evaluate risks based on two factors:

1. Likelihood of occurrence
2. Potential business impact

These factors are combined to determine an overall risk rating.

---

## Likelihood Scale

| Rating | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Low | Unlikely to occur during normal operations |
| Medium | Could occur under certain operational conditions |
| High | Likely to occur without stronger controls |

---

## Impact Scale

| Rating | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Low | Minor inconvenience or limited operational impact |
| Medium | Noticeable operational disruption or limited data exposure |
| High | Significant operational, financial, or reputational impact |

---

## Risk Rating Matrix

| Likelihood / Impact | Low Impact | Medium Impact | High Impact |
|---------------------|------------|---------------|-------------|
| Low Likelihood | Low | Low | Medium |
| Medium Likelihood | Low | Medium | High |
| High Likelihood | Medium | High | High |

---

## Usage

During risk assessments, each identified risk is evaluated based on:

- The likelihood that the event may occur
- The potential impact if the event occurs

The resulting rating helps prioritise remediation efforts and resource allocation.
