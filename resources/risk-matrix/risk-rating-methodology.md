# Risk Rating Methodology

This methodology describes how risks are evaluated and prioritised during a technology risk assessment.

---

## Risk Assessment Approach

The assessment process typically involves the following steps:

1. Identify technology assets and operational dependencies
2. Identify potential threats and control weaknesses
3. Assess likelihood of occurrence
4. Assess potential business impact
5. Evaluate existing controls
6. Assign a risk rating
7. Recommend mitigation actions

---

## Likelihood Assessment

Likelihood reflects the probability that a risk event could occur.

Factors considered may include:

- Known vulnerabilities
- Control maturity
- Operational practices
- External threat environment

---

## Impact Assessment

Impact reflects the severity of consequences if the risk occurs.

Impact may affect:

- System availability
- Data confidentiality
- Data integrity
- Operational continuity
- Financial or reputational standing

---

## Risk Rating Calculation

Risk ratings are determined using a qualitative assessment based on the combination of likelihood and impact.

Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact

The resulting rating is categorised as:

| Risk Level | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Low | Acceptable risk requiring minimal action |
| Medium | Moderate risk requiring monitoring and mitigation |
| High | Significant risk requiring remediation |

---

## Risk Treatment

Once risks are identified, organisations typically respond through one of the following strategies:

- Mitigate the risk through stronger controls
- Transfer the risk (for example through contracts or insurance)
- Avoid the risk by changing processes
- Accept the risk if within tolerance levels
